about us

The Club Europeo is an officially registered association with a statute approved on 9th March 1999 by the General Assembly of its members. The Club, however, has been in existence for over thirty years, having been founded at the same time as the Euratom research centre, now the Joint Research Centre, when the original members, wives of the first Commission employees in Ispra, started to meet and organise a club. The Club’s legal domicile is the JRC of the European Commission in Ispra c/o the Club House, Via Esperia.


The Club was founded to foster social contact and to help its members of many different nationalities, cultures and languages to integrate. To this end, the Club has developed a range of activities, both cultural and recreational, to provide opportunities for contact and integration. The Club’s activities are in no way influenced by politics, religion or race. The Club’s activities are never organised on a profit-making basis. Members who offer their services to the Club do so without payment

The Club Europeo has 500 members with links to the JRC, the European School and the area around Ispra who take part in a range of social and recreative activities. Members can invite guests to take part in club activities by paying a contribution on their behalf. The contribution should not exceed the actual expenses incurred.

The Club organises a wide range of free language classes for its members. The purpose of the classes is to promote social contact and cultural exchange among members. All teachers give their services voluntarily, therefore it is important to remember that the Club is not, in fact, a language school. For every course, the procedure to follow (books, holiday breaks, communication of absences of either teacher or pupils etc.) will be agreed between the teacher and pupils. Members can attend as many different courses as they wish. Other courses may be organised during the year. If so, they will be announced in the newsletter. Members interested in enrolling for a course can do so by giving their name and paying the sum requested, if any. A group of members meets regularly for exercise sessions in the gymnasium at the Club House. Members must pay the annual membership fee to the ‘Budokai’ for being in rule with the insurance covers. The Club also organises excursions and outings to places of interest and occasional short walks. Volunteer teachers and new ideas for courses of any kind are also welcome! Just contact the Committee or put forward your idea during the Annual General Meeting.
